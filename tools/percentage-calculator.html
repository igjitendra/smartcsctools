Basic Percentage: What is X% of Y?

Example: 20% of 150 = 30

Formula: (20 ÷ 100) × 150 = 30

Percentage Increase: From X to Y

Example: From 100 to 150 = 50% increase

Formula: [(150-100)÷100] × 100 = 50%

Percentage Decrease: From X to Y

Example: From 200 to 150 = 25% decrease

Formula: [(200-150)÷200] × 100 = 25%

Find Percentage: X is what % of Y?

Example: 30 is what % of 150? = 20%

Formula: (30 ÷ 150) × 100 = 20%

Percentage Calculator — Sab Kuch Ek Jagah

Marks, discount, GST, salary hike ya population growth — percentage har jagah kaam aata hai. Ye tool 4 sabse common percentage calculations ko step-by-step solve karta hai.

Ye tool kya karta hai?

Percentage Calculator me 4 modes hain: Basic % (X% of Y), Percentage Increase, Percentage Decrease, aur Find % (X is what % of Y). Har calculation ke saath aapko formula, step-by-step solution, live visual bar aur real-world examples milte hain. Sab kuch aapke browser me chalta hai — data kahin upload nahi hota.

Kyun use karein?

  • Turant answer: Type karte hi live result, calculate dabane ki bhi zarurat nahi.
  • Samajh aata hai: Sirf answer nahi, poora step-by-step method dikhta hai.
  • Har case cover: Basic, increase, decrease aur part-of-whole — sab ek tool me.
  • Free & private: No signup, no ads-wall, no data upload.

Kiske liye best hai?

  • Students: Marks percentage, test score aur maths homework ke liye.
  • CSC VLEs & shopkeepers: Discount, GST, commission aur profit/loss quickly.
  • Job aspirants: Aptitude aur reasoning ke percentage questions practice ke liye.

Pro tips

  • Marks % ke liye “Find %” mode: obtained marks = Part, total = Whole.
  • Discount % ke liye “Decrease” mode: old price se new price.
  • Ctrl+Enter dabakar turant calculate, Esc se reset kar sakte hain.
